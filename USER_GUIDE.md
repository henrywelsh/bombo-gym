# Bombo Gym — User Guide

Welcome to Bombo Gym, a personal training tracker built around the **Lean Mass & Athletic Conditioning Program**. This guide walks you through every feature so you can hit the ground running.

---

## Getting Started

### Signing In
1. Open the app in your browser
2. Click **Sign in with Google** and complete the sign-in flow
3. On your first visit, you'll be taken to **Settings** to complete your profile

### First-Time Setup (Settings)
Before anything else works properly, fill in your profile:

- **Program Start Date** — the date you plan to (or did) begin the program. This is the most important field: the app uses it to figure out which month of the program you're on and what your current exercise targets should be. If you haven't started yet, pick your intended start date.
- **Height** — enter in inches (e.g. 6'3" = 75 inches). Used to calculate your BMI.
- **Current Weight** — your starting body weight in pounds.
- **Target Weight** — defaults to 200 lbs per the program goal.

Click **Save Profile** when done. You can update any of these fields at any time.

## The Program at a Glance

The app is built around a 12-month kettlebell strength and conditioning plan. Here's the weekly structure:

| Day | Strength Focus | Peloton |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Upper Pull + Core | Endurance (30–45 min) |
| Tuesday | Lower Body + Conditioning | HIIT/Power (20–30 min) |
| Wednesday | Upper Push + Core | Endurance (30–45 min) |
| Thursday | Lower Body + Conditioning | HIIT/Power (20–30 min) |
| Friday | Full-body Power + Pull | Endurance (30–45 min) |
| Weekend | Rest / Sport (volleyball, surfing) | — |

**Daily flow for each training day:**
1. Mobility warm-up · 15 min
2. Strength session · 45 min
3. Conditioning (Peloton or sport) · 30–60 min
4. Mobility cool-down · 15–20 min

## Today (Dashboard)

The **Today** tab is your home base. Every time you open the app, start here.

### What you'll see
- **Today's date** and your current program month
- **Today's split** — the strength focus for the day (e.g. "Upper Pull + Core")
- **Exercise cards** — one per exercise scheduled for today, plus the daily staples (Swings, Halos, Prying Goblet Squat)

### Exercise Cards
Each card shows:
- **Current target** — the weight, sets, and reps you should be working toward right now, based on where you are in the 12-month progression
- **Next milestone** — what you're building toward (e.g. "Month 6: 80 lb · 10×8")
- A **green dot** if you've already logged that exercise today

### Weekends
On Saturday and Sunday the app shows a rest day screen. Strength training runs Monday–Friday only.

## Logging a Session

After your workout, go to the **Log** tab to record what you did.

### How to log
1. The date defaults to today — change it if you're logging a past session
2. Tap the exercise buttons at the bottom to add exercises to your log. Each one pre-fills with your current milestone targets as a starting point
3. Edit the **Sets**, **Reps**, and **Weight** fields to match what you actually did
4. Add optional **Notes** (form cues, how it felt, what to work on)
5. Tap **Save Session**

### Tips
- You can log any combination of exercises — you're not locked into the day's split
- If you already logged today and want to make a correction, simply change the values and save again — it won't create duplicates
- Logging consistently is what makes the Progress charts useful

## Progress

The **Progress** tab shows how your strength and body weight are trending over time.

### Strength Charts
- Use the exercise buttons to switch between exercises
- The chart shows your **logged max weight per date** as a line
- The **progression bar** at the top shows where you are in the 12-month program
- Below the chart, the **milestone table** shows the program's targets at months 0, 3, 6, 9, and 12 — months you've already passed are highlighted in amber

### Body Weight Chart
- Shows your logged body weight over time
- A green dashed line marks your target weight (200 lbs by default)

### Muscle Percentage
- If you log muscle percentage in your measurements, a separate chart appears here

## Nutrition

The **Nutrition** tab covers three things: supplements, meal planning, and body measurements.

### Daily Supplement Checklist
At the top of the page, your active supplements appear as checkboxes. Check each one off as you take it throughout the day. The checklist resets each day.

To add or edit supplements, go to **Settings**.

### Weekly Meal Planner
The meal planner is a 7-day grid with five meal slots per day:
- Breakfast
- Lunch
- Snack
- Dinner
- Post-Workout

**To add a meal:**
1. Tap any cell in the grid
2. Optionally select a **meal template** from the dropdown to auto-fill the details
3. Type a description of what you're eating
4. Fill in macros if you want to track them (calories, protein, carbs, fat)
5. Tap **Save**

**Daily macro targets (from the program):**
- Calories: ~3,400 kcal/day
- Protein: ~220 g/day
- Hydration: 90–180 oz water/day

The column header for each day shows a running calorie total as you fill in meals.

**To clear a meal:** tap the cell → tap **Clear**

**Week navigation:** use the **‹** and **›** arrows to move between weeks. Each week's plan is saved independently.

### Meal Templates
Templates let you save frequently eaten meals so you can quickly fill in the planner without retyping.

**To create a template:**
1. Scroll to the Meal Templates section
2. Click **+ Template**
3. Give it a name (e.g. "Standard Breakfast"), a description, and optionally fill in macros
4. Save

Templates appear in the dropdown whenever you edit a meal cell.

### Body Measurements
Log your weight and muscle percentage periodically (weekly is ideal) to track your progress toward 200 lbs.

- **Weight (lb)** — required
- **Muscle %** — optional, if you have a way to measure it (smart scale, DEXA, etc.)
- **BMI** — calculated automatically from your height and the weight you log

Your last logged measurement is shown at the top of the section. A full history table shows the last 10 entries.

## Settings

### Profile
Update your start date, height, weight, and goal weight at any time. Changing the start date immediately updates all exercise targets across the app.

### Supplements & Habits
Add anything you take or do regularly:
- **Name** — e.g. "Creatine Monohydrate"
- **Dose** — e.g. "5g"
- **Frequency** — e.g. "Daily"
- **Notes** — optional reminders

Click **Import defaults from program** to add the three supplements recommended in the program (creatine, fish oil, vitamin D) in one tap.

To remove a supplement, click **×** next to it. It won't delete your past compliance logs.

### Sign Out
At the bottom of Settings. Your data is saved to the cloud and will be there when you sign back in.

## Mobility Routines

### Pre-Strength Warm-up (15 min)
1. Shoulder Dislocates: 2×10
2. Hip Circles: 2×10 each side
3. Cat/Cow: 1 min
4. Prying Goblet Squat Hold: 2×10 deep breath holds
5. Ankle Dorsiflexion Stretch: 2×30 sec each side

### Post-Strength Cool-down (15–20 min)
1. Hamstring Stretch: 2×30 sec
2. Pigeon Pose: 2×30 sec each side
3. Thoracic Rotations: 2×10 each side
4. Shoulder Halos w/light KB: 2×10
5. Child's Pose w/side stretch: 2×30 sec

### Optional Morning Routine (10 min)
Spinal Twists · Cat/Cow · Shoulder Band Stretch · Hip Openers

### Evening Wind-Down (10–15 min)
Deep Hamstring Stretch · Adductor Stretch · Supine Twists · Pectoral Stretch

## Key Program Principles

- **Prioritize form over load** — especially with the labrum and ankle. Don't chase weight if your form breaks down.
- **Progress weekly** — add weight or reps each week, not each session.
- **Daily staples every day** — Swings, Halos, and Prying Goblet Squats are done every training day regardless of the split.
- **Avoid stacking heavy lower-body days** — if your back or ankle is fatigued, skip or lighten swings and lower-body work.
- **Deload every 4–6 weeks** — reduce strength volume by 40–50% for one week to let your body recover.
- **Nutrition is non-negotiable** — 3,400 kcal/day and 220g protein. You won't gain mass without eating enough.
